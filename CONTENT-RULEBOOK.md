# Content Rulebook

Shared editorial standards for all Cosmic Variety Show posts. Updated as we learn.

## Voice & Attribution
- Every post gets a **byline under the title**: author name and title (e.g., "by Carl Sagan, The Cosmic Evangelist")
- When quoting another voice within a post, name them clearly

## Language Rules
- **No em dashes.** They are an AI tell in modern writing. Use periods, commas, colons, or restructure the sentence.
- **No negative correlation overuse.** Same reason. Reads as machine-generated.
- **No superlatives unless true and verifiable.** "The greatest reduction in poverty in history" is true (data backs it). "The most incredible thing ever" is hype. Facts do the work. If the number is surprising, the number is enough.
- **ELI5 all concepts.** Every new term gets a plain-English definition the first time it appears. If a fifth grader cannot follow the sentence, rewrite it.
  - **Trim Tab**: A tiny rudder on the back of the big rudder on a ship. Push the tiny one, the big one moves, and the whole ship turns. Our version: a small question that makes you realize you were wrong about something, and that little surprise changes how you think about everything connected to it.
  - **Shallow-right**: When you have the right answer but do not actually understand it. Like knowing the capital of France is Paris but having no idea where it is on a map. You would pass the test, but you could not find your way there.
  - **Reconnection protocol**: A way to rebuild shared reality between people who have been sealed inside separate information bubbles. The Trim Tab does this by giving people a shared experience of being surprised by the same fact.

## Structure Rules
- **Each post must work standalone AND in sequence.** A reader may arrive at any post first. Do not assume they have read the others.
- **Sequencing references must be accurate.** If post 2 mentions something from post 4, fix it or remove it.
- **End with a forward hook** when possible, but do not reference content that has not been published yet.

## Formatting
- Short paragraphs. Web readers scan.
- Bold key sentences sparingly. Not every paragraph.
- Headers break up long pieces. Aim for a new header every 3-4 paragraphs.
- Quotes get blockquote formatting.

## Quality Checks
- Before publishing: does it pass the Feynman honesty test? (Is anything exaggerated or misleading?)
- Before publishing: does it pass the Sagan communicability test? (Would a curious fourteen-year-old finish reading it?)
- Before publishing: does it pass the Fuller structural test? (Does every section bear load? Can anything be removed without the piece collapsing?)
